State of Pennsylvania Monument

Situated in Gettysburg National Military Park, this is the largest state monument on the Gettysburg Battlefield. It commemorates the 34,530 Pennsylvania soldiers who fought in the Battle of Gettysburg during the Civil War. The monument features a grand dome and bronze statues of notable figures.

Flight 93 National Memorial

Located in Stoystown, this memorial honors the passengers and crew of United Airlines Flight 93 who heroically thwarted a terrorist attack on September 11, 2001. The site includes a visitor center, the Wall of Names, and the Tower of Voices, a 93-foot tall musical instrument with 40 wind chimes representing each person lost.

PA State Police Historical Museum

Located in Hershey, this museum preserves and interprets the history of the Pennsylvania State Police. It serves as a lasting memorial for those who have served, especially those who gave their lives in the line of duty. The museum includes exhibits on the evolution of the state police force and significant cases.

Memorial Park

Found in Lemoyne, this 8.1-acre park is perfect for a leisurely walk or a family outing. It features sports facilities, including basketball and baseball courts, and a swimming pool. The park is known for its beautiful tree-lined paths and serene atmosphere.
